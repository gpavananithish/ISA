Worlds Beyond Our Own

Our solar system is a vast and wondrous place, home to eight unique planets orbiting our Sun. Beyond that, countless exoplanets orbit distant stars, some of which may even have the right conditions for life. Explore our celestial neighbors, both near and far.

Planets of Our Solar System

Mercury

The smallest planet in our solar system and nearest to the Sun, Mercury is a world of extremes, with scorching hot days and freezing cold nights.

  • Composition: Rocky surface with a large metallic core.
  • Distance from Sun: ~58 million km
  • Diameter: 4,880 km
  • Natural Satellites: 0

Venus

Often called Earth's "sister planet," Venus is shrouded in a thick, toxic atmosphere that traps heat, making it the hottest planet in our solar system.

  • Composition: Rocky mantle, thick Carbon Dioxide atmosphere, Sulfuric Acid clouds.
  • Distance from Sun: ~108 million km
  • Diameter: 12,104 km
  • Natural Satellites: 0

Earth

Our home planet is the only place we know of so far thatâ€™s inhabited by living things. It's the only world in our solar system with liquid water on the surface.

  • Composition: Rock, liquid water, Nitrogen-Oxygen atmosphere.
  • Distance from Sun: ~150 million km
  • Diameter: 12,742 km
  • Natural Satellites: 1

Mars

The "Red Planet" is a dusty, cold, desert world with a very thin atmosphere. Mars is one of the most explored bodies in our solar system.

  • Composition: Rock, iron oxide dust (rust), thin Carbon Dioxide atmosphere.
  • Distance from Sun: ~228 million km
  • Diameter: 6,779 km
  • Natural Satellites: 2

Jupiter

The largest planet in our solar system, a gas giant with a Great Red Spot.

  • Composition: Gas Giant (Hydrogen, Helium). No solid surface.
  • Distance from Sun: ~778 million km
  • Diameter: 139,820 km
  • Natural Satellites: 95

Saturn

Adorned with a dazzling, complex system of icy rings, Saturn is unique in our solar system.

  • Composition: Gas Giant (Hydrogen, Helium). Rings of ice and rock.
  • Distance from Sun: ~1.4 billion km
  • Diameter: 116,460 km
  • Natural Satellites: 146

Uranus

An ice giant that rotates on its side, giving it extreme seasons.

  • Composition: Ice Giant (Water, Methane, Ammonia fluids). Hydrogen-Helium atmosphere.
  • Distance from Sun: ~2.9 billion km
  • Diameter: 50,724 km
  • Natural Satellites: 28

Neptune

The windiest planet in our solar system, dark, cold, and whipped by supersonic winds.

  • Composition: Ice Giant (Water, Ammonia, Methane).
  • Distance from Sun: ~4.5 billion km
  • Diameter: 49,244 km
  • Natural Satellites: 16

Pluto

A dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt, a ring of bodies beyond Neptune.

  • Composition: Rock core, mantle of water ice, surface of frozen nitrogen and methane.
  • Distance from Sun: ~5.9 billion km
  • Diameter: 2,377 km
  • Natural Satellites: 5

Potentially Habitable Exoplanets

Proxima Centauri b

Our closest known exoplanet, orbiting the red dwarf star Proxima Centauri. It lies within the star's habitable zone, where liquid water could exist.

  • Distance from Earth: ~4.2 light-years
  • Size: ~1.17 times Earth's mass
  • Star System: Alpha Centauri

TRAPPIST-1e

One of seven Earth-sized planets orbiting an ultra-cool dwarf star. It is considered one of the most promising exoplanets for habitability.

  • Distance from Earth: ~40 light-years
  • Size: Similar to Earth
  • Star System: TRAPPIST-1
