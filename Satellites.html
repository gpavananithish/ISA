India's Eyes in the Sky

From its first satellite, Aryabhata, to its ambitious interplanetary missions, India has a rich history of satellite technology. The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) has launched over 100 satellites serving various purposes, including communication, remote sensing, navigation, and scientific exploration. Here are some of the most notable missions.

Planetary & Space Exploration

Chandrayaan-3

India's historic mission that successfully landed on the Moon's south pole, making India the fourth nation to land on the Moon and the first to reach the south pole.

  • Launch Date: July 14, 2023
  • Reached Destination: August 23, 2023 (Lunar South Pole)
  • Type: Lunar Lander & Rover
  • Components: Propulsion Module, Vikram Lander, Pragyan Rover
  • Payloads: RAMBHA, ChaSTE, ILSA, APXS, LIBS
  • Goal: To demonstrate safe landing and roving on the lunar surface and conduct in-situ scientific experiments.

Aditya-L1

India's first dedicated solar mission, placed in a halo orbit around the Lagrange point 1 (L1) of the Sun-Earth system.

  • Launch Date: September 2, 2023
  • Reached Destination: January 6, 2024 (L1 Halo Orbit)
  • Type: Solar Observatory
  • Components: I-1000 Bus
  • Payloads: VELC, SUIT, SoLEXS, HEL1OS, ASPEX, PAPA, MAG
  • Goal: To study the solar corona, photosphere, chromosphere, and solar wind.

Mangalyaan (Mars Orbiter Mission)

India's first interplanetary mission, making it the first Asian nation to reach Martian orbit and the first nation in the world to do so on its maiden attempt.

  • Launch Date: November 5, 2013
  • Reached Destination: September 24, 2014 (Mars Orbit)
  • Type: Mars Orbiter
  • Components: I-1K Bus Structure
  • Payloads: MCC, TIS, MSM, LAP, MENCA
  • Goal: To explore Mars' surface features, morphology, mineralogy and Martian atmosphere.

Chandrayaan-2

A highly complex mission comprising an Orbiter, Lander, and Rover to explore the unexplored South Pole of the Moon.

  • Launch Date: July 22, 2019
  • Reached Destination: August 20, 2019 (Lunar Orbit)
  • Type: Lunar Orbiter, Lander & Rover
  • Components: Orbiter, Vikram Lander, Pragyan Rover
  • Payloads: Terrain Mapping Camera-2, CLASS, XSM
  • Goal: To map the lunar surface and study its exosphere and mineralogy.

Chandrayaan-1

India's first mission to the Moon. The mission was a major boost to India's space program, as it helped discover water molecules on the Moon.

  • Launch Date: October 22, 2008
  • Reached Destination: November 8, 2008 (Lunar Orbit)
  • Type: Lunar Orbiter
  • Components: Modified I-1K Bus, Moon Impact Probe (MIP)
  • Payloads: TMC, HySI, LLRI, HEX
  • Goal: High-resolution mapping of the lunar surface and mineral distribution.

AstroSat

India's first dedicated multi-wavelength space observatory. It enables the simultaneous observation of the universe in ultraviolet, optical, and X-ray bands.

  • Launch Date: September 28, 2015
  • Reached Destination: September 28, 2015 (LEO)
  • Type: Space Observatory
  • Components: IRS Bus
  • Payloads: UVIT, LAXPC, SXT, CZTI, SSM
  • Goal: To study black holes, neutron stars, and star formation.

Earth Observation & Navigation

Cartosat Series

A series of Earth observation satellites indigenous to India. The Cartosat series is a part of the Indian Remote Sensing Programme.

  • Launch Date: May 5, 2005 (Cartosat-1)
  • Reached Destination: Sun-Synchronous Orbit
  • Type: Earth Observation
  • Components: Panchromatic & Multispectral Cameras
  • Goal: Cartography, urban planning, coastal land use, and utility management.

RISAT (Radar Imaging Satellite)

A series of Indian radar imaging reconnaissance satellites. They provide all-weather surveillance using synthetic aperture radars (SAR).

  • Launch Date: April 26, 2012 (RISAT-1)
  • Reached Destination: Sun-Synchronous Orbit
  • Type: Radar Imaging
  • Components: Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR)
  • Goal: Earth observation, disaster management, and border surveillance.

NavIC (IRNSS)

Navigation with Indian Constellation (NavIC) is an autonomous regional satellite navigation system that provides accurate real-time positioning.

  • Launch Date: July 1, 2013 (IRNSS-1A)
  • Reached Destination: Geosynchronous/Geostationary Orbit
  • Type: Navigation
  • Components: Rubidium Atomic Clocks, L5 & S-band Payload
  • Goal: To provide standard positioning service to users in India and the surrounding region.

INSAT Series

A series of multi-purpose geo-stationary satellites launched by ISRO to satisfy the telecommunications, broadcasting, and meteorology needs of India.

  • Launch Date: August 30, 1983 (INSAT-1B)
  • Reached Destination: Geostationary Orbit
  • Type: Communication & Meteorology
  • Components: C/Ku/S-band Transponders, VHRR
  • Goal: Telecommunications, broadcasting, and weather forecasting.

GSAT Series

India's indigenously developed communications satellites, used for digital audio, data and video broadcasting.

  • Launch Date: April 18, 2001 (GSAT-1)
  • Reached Destination: Geostationary Orbit
  • Type: Communication
  • Components: High-power Transponders (Ku, Ka bands)
  • Goal: To provide communication services for tele-education, tele-medicine, and military applications.

Experimental & Early Satellites

Aryabhata

Named after the famous Indian astronomer, this was India's first-ever satellite, marking the nation's entry into the space age.

  • Launch Date: April 19, 1975
  • Reached Destination: Low Earth Orbit
  • Type: Experimental
  • Components: X-ray Astronomy & Solar Physics Payloads
  • Goal: To gain experience in building and operating a satellite in space.

Bhaskara-I

India's first experimental remote sensing satellite. It carried TV cameras and microwave radiometers.

  • Launch Date: June 7, 1979
  • Reached Destination: Low Earth Orbit
  • Type: Earth Observation (Experimental)
  • Components: TV Cameras, Microwave Radiometers
  • Goal: To collect data on hydrology, forestry, and oceanography.

Rohini RS-1

The first satellite successfully launched by the indigenous launch vehicle SLV-3, making India the sixth nation to possess space-faring capability.

  • Launch Date: July 18, 1980
  • Reached Destination: Low Earth Orbit
  • Type: Experimental
  • Components: Digital Sun Sensor, Magnetometer
  • Goal: To evaluate the performance of the SLV-3 launch vehicle.
